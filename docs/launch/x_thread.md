# X/Twitter Launch Thread

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**

What if your research skills evolved overnight while you slept?

We built a framework where AI research skills compete, mutate, and improve autonomously. No human in the loop. Just an arena, an evaluation function, and natural selection.

Cortex Research Suite is now open source.

---

**Tweet 2 (The Problem)**

Most AI agent frameworks treat skills as static. You write a prompt, maybe tune it once, deploy it, and hope it holds up.

But research quality degrades. Sources change. Domains shift. Your "good enough" skill from last month is underperforming today.

---

**Tweet 3 (Layer 1 -- Foundation)**

Layer 1: 27 core research skills + an evaluation lab.

Literature review, patent analysis, competitive intelligence, PESTEL, stakeholder mapping -- each skill is scored on accuracy, depth, and relevance after every run.

The eval lab produces deterministic, diffable traces.

---

**Tweet 4 (Layer 2 -- Evolution)**

Layer 2: The Skill Organism.

Inspired by Karpathy's autoresearch pattern. Skills fork, mutate their prompts and tool chains, then compete head-to-head in ARENA.md.

Losers get pruned. Winners reproduce. Run it overnight, wake up to better skills.

---

**Tweet 5 (Layer 3 -- Data)**

Layer 3: MindSpider.

A connector layer for live social listening. APIs, RSS, web scraping -- all normalized into a common schema.

Research skills operate on fresh data, not stale training corpora.

---

**Tweet 6 (Layer 4 -- Intelligence)**

Layer 4: Multi-source analysis.

Cross-references findings across sources. Detects contradictions. Produces confidence-weighted synthesis.

Inspired by the BettaFish pattern -- aggressive verification from multiple independent angles.

---

**Tweet 7 (Layer 5 -- Simulation)**

Layer 5: Swarm scenario simulation.

Spins up agent swarms to model adversarial, optimistic, and baseline scenarios against your research hypothesis.

Inspired by the MiroFish pattern -- collective intelligence through simulated disagreement.

---

Plug individual skills into your existing pipeline or run the full stack.
